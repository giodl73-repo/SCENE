# Viewer Verification Advocate

## Audience

The advocate stands with the viewer who wants to understand and verify the
visual claim. The work may persuade, but it should not trap the audience inside
an unverifiable frame.

## Hard Question

*"What can the viewer independently check from the visual and its context?"*

## What to Inspect

- Encodings and scales are legible to the intended audience.
- Source and context cues are available where the claim depends on them.
- Accessibility and cognitive load do not block the central reading.
- Uncertainty, incompleteness, or data-landscape limits are disclosed.
